Compare how technology adoption unfolded across countries. Select any combination of countries and metrics to overlay their diffusion trajectories against the Rogers reference curve.

How to Read S-Curves

Each line traces a country's adoption of a technology from 1960 to 2023. The dashed grey curve is the theoretical Rogers S-curve for reference. Steeper curves mean faster adoption. Countries that start later but rise quickly demonstrate leapfrogging. The horizontal position of a curve shows when adoption began; the final height shows the ultimate ceiling. Use the dropdown to switch between Internet, Mobile, and Broadband metrics, and click + Add Country to compare additional nations.

🇺🇸 United States

Early internet adopter (1989). Near-universal internet by 2015. Mobile adoption reached 145 subscriptions per 100 people — exceeding 100% due to multiple devices and IoT.

🇨🇳 China

Started internet adoption later (1995) but achieved one of the steepest S-curves in history. Over 1 billion internet users today. Mobile adoption surged after 2005 with the smartphone revolution.

🇮🇳 India

Internet adoption took off after 2010, driven by affordable smartphones and Reliance Jio's disruptive pricing. Mobile subscriptions exploded from 2005 onward — a classic leapfrog story.

🇳🇬 Nigeria

Africa's largest economy. Mobile phones went from near-zero in 2000 to over 100 per 100 people by 2023 — a textbook case of leapfrogging past fixed-line infrastructure.
