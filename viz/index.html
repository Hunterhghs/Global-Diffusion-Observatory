About the Data

Data covers 101 countries from 1960 to 2023. Technology adoption follows Rogers' S-curve diffusion pattern — early adopters (high-income nations) led, with developing countries catching up over time. The digital divide is visible in the spread between regions.

GDP per capita (PPP, constant 2017 $) on the x-axis vs. technology adoption on the y-axis. Bubble size = population. Color = region. This format — pioneered by Hans Rosling's Gapminder — reveals the relationship between economic development and technology diffusion.

Diffusion Theory

Rogers' Diffusion of Innovations theory describes how new technologies spread through a population in an S-curve pattern: innovators → early adopters → early majority → late majority → laggards. This visualization lets you watch that process unfold globally.

Key patterns to observe: leapfrogging (mobile phones bypassing fixed lines in Africa), the digital divide (Sub-Saharan Africa vs. Europe), and convergence (developing nations catching up as costs fall and infrastructure improves).
